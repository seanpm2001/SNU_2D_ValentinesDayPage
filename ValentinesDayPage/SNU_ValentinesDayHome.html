About valentines day (article)

Valentines Day is a holiday on the 14th of February. It was originally a Christian holiday based on the story of Saint Valentine, but it has become a global holiday themed around love, along with still being a Christian celebration.

Valentines Day is always celebrated on the 14th of February.

Global acceptance

Valentines day is not globally accepted. In countries such as Iran, the celebration of the holiday is outright banned due to compatibility issues with the Islam religion.

Valentines day in Iran

Valentines day is mostly banned and discouraged in Iran due to its incompatibility with Islam, and of it being a western tradition.

In Iran, the Sepandarmazgan, or Esfandegan, is a festival where people express love towards their mothers and wives, and it is also a celebration of earth in ancient Persian culture. The Iranian government is pushing the Sepandarmazgan in place of Valentines Day

In China, Valentine's Day is called lovers' festival. The "Chinese Valentine's Day" is the Qixi Festival, celebrated on the seventh day of the seventh month of the lunar calendar. It commemorates a day on which a legendary cowherder and weaving maid are allowed to be together. In Chinese culture, there is an older observance related to lovers, called "The Night of Sevens" (Chinese: 七夕; pinyin: Qi Xi). According to the legend, the Cowherd star and the Weaver Maid star are normally separated by the Milky Way (silvery river) but are allowed to meet by crossing it on the 7th day of the 7th month of the Chinese calendar.
